---
title: The Race to the Bottom (and Why Speed is the New Smart)
date: "2026-06-15T11:00:00.000Z"
description: "AI models aren't getting smarter as fast as they used to, but they're getting a hell of a lot faster and cheaper."
---

We've spent the last few years obsessing over whether AI could pass the Bar exam or write a better novel than a human. But June 2026 is showing us that the "intelligence" war has cooled down, and the "speed" war is just getting started. The release of Gemini 3.5 Flash and GPT-5.5 Instant marks a clear shift in strategy from the big labs. They aren't trying to build a digital god anymore; they're trying to build a digital engine that can run 300 tokens per second without breaking the bank.

If you look at the specs for Gemini 3.5 Flash, it’s hitting nearly 290 tokens per second. That’s fast enough to be effectively instantaneous. We’re moving away from the era of "waiting for the chatbot to type" and into the era of "agentic infrastructure." When a model is this fast and this cheap—$1.50 per million tokens—it changes the math on what you can actually build. You don't just use it to write an email; you use it to run thousands of tiny tasks in the background of your business without ever thinking about the cost.

OpenAI is playing a slightly different game with GPT-5.5 Instant. It’s slower than Gemini, but it’s holding onto a lead in deep reasoning and factual accuracy. They’re positioning it for the "high stakes" stuff—legal, medical, and financial work where you’d rather wait an extra second than get a hallucination. But even they are feeling the pressure to commoditize. The hallucination rate is dropping, but the real story is the 90% discount on cached inputs. They know that the only way to win is to make AI so cheap that it becomes as invisible as electricity.

This is the commoditization phase of the industry. The "wow" factor of a smart chatbot is gone. Now, it’s about who can provide the most reliable, fastest throughput for the lowest price. It might not be as exciting as a "philosophical split" or a "new era of intelligence," but it’s a lot more practical. We're finally moving past the hype and into the plumbing. And if history is any guide, the people who build the best plumbing are the ones who actually end up owning the future.
